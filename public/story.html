Our story

The people who matter don't drift on purpose.

Space started with a quiet observation: the relationships we lose are rarely the ones we stopped caring about. They're the ones we never quite got around to.

Think of someone you love but haven't spoken to in months. Chances are, nothing went wrong. There was no falling out. You meant to call. They meant to call. The right moment never quite arrived.

This is how closeness fades. Not in an argument, but somewhere between I miss them and I'll reach out later.

We think the problem has been misnamed. It isn't people drifting apart. It's the small moments that never quite line up.

It was never about caring

What looks like people growing apart is usually something quieter: the friction of figuring out when to reach out.

You wonder if they're busy. If it's a good time. If a message after this long will feel strange. So you wait. And the better moment rarely arrives.

The tools weren't built for this

Most ways of staying in touch assume one of two things: that you're always reachable, or that you'll always be the one to reach out first. Life rarely works that neatly.

Sometimes the time is there, but the energy isn't. Sometimes the energy is there, but the timing isn't. That's the gap we kept noticing.

So we made something smaller

Instead of asking you to plan, Space lets you signal. You open the app and let the people close to you know you have a little room right now. That's the whole product.

They can reach out, or not. There's no request to answer, no conversation to maintain, no expectation on either side. When your time is up, the signal quietly turns itself off.

It's like leaving a porch light on. You're not asking anyone to come over. You're simply letting the people close to you know you're around.

What we believe

Connection should feel easier than it usually does. Attention is precious, so every notification should earn the interruption. And once you're talking to someone you care about, Space has already done its job.

That's why it stays small on purpose. And why we intend to keep it that way.

Leave your light on.
